1. What?

An overview of Vivarium

Sun Oct  4 2015
Vivarium is an artificial life simulation. The simulation consists of walls, food, and creatures. FIG. 1.1
 FIG. 1.2
 A world in vivarium has static non-moving walls. These walls line the edge of a world, and additional walls are scattered around the middle of a world. Figure 1.1 shows an example arrangement of walls. A Vivarium world also has food. Food is a resource that spawns in empty space with a low probability every tick. Figure 1.2 shows a piece of food. FIG. 1.3
 The main focus of a Vivarium simulation is the creatures. Figure 1.3 shows a male (red) and female (blue) creature. Creatures in the simulation have a genome that is used to build a neurological model for the creature. These creatures live, eat, breed, and die, all using a digital brain that is derived from their genome. On each tick of the simulation, each creature receives a set of inputs. These inputs are fed into the brain of the creature, and the creature picks an action to perform. In the current visualizer (watch), each creature receives a very limited set of inputs: Whether it is female, whether it is facing a wall, whether it is facing food, whether it is facing another creature, and the percent energy it has left. Creature brains are small neural networks, and the weights between each node in the neural network are a gene that is passed on, recombined, and mutated over the generations. It's through many small iterations and selections that these creatures will, given enough time, learn to use the resources that their world gives them much more efficiently.
